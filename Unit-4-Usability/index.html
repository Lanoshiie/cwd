Google Maps Usability Report

Google Maps Usability Report

A usability test conducted on Google Maps with five tasks, evaluating how easily users can navigate and interact with the platform.

Usability Tasks

Task 1 — Add a Location

Time: 2 minutes

The subject was asked to find where they could add a location of their choice and fill in the location details. They chose the UMW Hospital and navigated to the "Suggest an Edit" feature, where they added details about how the location was walkable from the University of Mary Washington. They did not submit the changes to avoid making real alterations to the map. No hints were given.

Task 2 — Find the Closest Museum and Exact Distance

Time: 30 seconds

The subject was asked to find the closest museum and its exact distance from their current location. They searched "museum" directly in the search bar and quickly identified the Fredericksburg Museum as the nearest result, approximately 25 minutes away from the UCC building on UMW's campus. The task was completed with no difficulty and no hints were given.

Task 3 — Find the Closest Olive Garden and Closing Time

Time: 1 minute

The subject was asked to locate the nearest Olive Garden and find out what time it closes. They searched "Olive Garden" in the search bar and found the closest location without difficulty. The closing time of 10pm was visible on the listing. No hints were given.

Task 4 — Walk Time from Olive Garden to Closest Target

Time: 1 minute 30 seconds

The subject was asked to find out how long it would take to walk from the Olive Garden to the closest Target. They clicked the directions icon within the Olive Garden listing and searched for Target, identifying the closest location and finding that the walk would take approximately 22 minutes. No hints were given.

Task 5 — Find People Waving at the Google Street View Camera

Time: 3 minutes

The subject was asked to find people waving at the Google Street View camera on Google Maps. This was the most challenging task of the session — the subject struggled to identify where to begin and spent considerable time exploring the interface without success. A hint was provided, suggesting they look in Dawson City, Yukon, after which they were able to navigate to Street View and locate people waving at the camera. This task highlighted how deeply buried certain Street View features are within the Google Maps interface.
